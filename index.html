Alice Roosevelt Longworth

The daughter of 26th President Theodore Roosevelt known for her wit and rambunctious nature.

Alice Roosevelt's Life

1884

Alice was born in New York City to parents Theodore Roosevelt (who would go on to be the 26th president of the United States) and Alice Hathaway Lee Roosevelt.

1901

Alice's father became president, and headstrong Alice became the center of attention. She did unheard of things such as wear pants, gamble, and race cars down the street.

1906

Alice married Ohio Representative Nicholas Longworth and became more of a politician than a socialite. Even after her husband's death, she maintained her social and political influence. She even campaigned against her 5th cousin Franklin Roosevelt.

1912–1970

She was well known for decades after her father's presidency. She was banned from the White House by President William Howard Taft for burying a voodoo doll of Taft's wife in the yard. She was also banned by President Woodrow Wilson for making a joke about him.

"I have a simple philosophy: Fill what's empty. Empty what's full. Scratch where it itches."
— Alice Roosevelt Longworth
